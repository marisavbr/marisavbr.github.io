(Bacon) Cheddar Chive Scones

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Very pleased with these savory scones. I did not put bacon in them because I used it all when making the quiche! I ended up replacing the heavy cream with butter + milk becuase I didnt want them to be as cakey. I used preshredded cheddar cheese but I reccomend getting a block of cheese and preparing it yourself so you can have better control over the size of the grate or cubes. Next time, I will add more cheese and green onions if I do not use bacon.

Notes :

1. Instead of using 3/4 up of heavy cream/whipping cream I made a mixture of 6 tbsp of butter + 6 tbsp of milk for a more tender crumb. I used Cream Scones vs. Butter Scones as a reference for this substitution. Make sure the mixture is cool before adding it to the flour!

2. I used about 1 tbsp more milk to get the dough to stick together better after the initial incorporation of the 3/4 cup of butter + milk mixture.

3. Brushed the scones with milk instead of cream prior to baking.

4. Baked for about 23 minutes.

Bacon, Cheddar + Green Onion Quiche

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

I was very happy with how this turned out! I am very excited to have the leftovers for breakfast. My one regret was not par-baking the pie dough for longer after I removed the pie weights. The bottom of the crust was still pretty soggy/doughy (Paul Hollywood would have been ashamed). I think another 4-5 minutes added to the par-bake would have resulted in a better crust. Overall, the recipe was very good and I did not make very many changes to it.

Notes :

1. I used about two times as much green onion than what was called for in the recipe.

2. Replaced the ham with turkey bacon. I cooked this prior to baking it in the quiche.

3. I cut back on the salt and only used ~1/2 a teaspoon.

3. Par-bake the pie dough before adding the egg mixture to avoid soggy crust. Instructions : How to Par-Bake Pie Crust.

Curry Chicken : The Woks of Life + Dinner then Dessert

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

I referenced these two recipes to make the curry chicken. I was very happy with the outcome of combining the recipes. I would like to try it with coconut milk/cream as a way to make it a bit more rich. Overall, I was satisfied with the meal and will be making this again in the near future. Update : adding coconut milk makes this more rich and velvety, and adds only slight hints of coconut to the over all flavor. I usually add around 1/4 - 1/2 of a cup of coconut milk.

Notes :

1. Cut chicken into bite-sized bits (~1 lb) + toss in soy sauce and corn starch mixture.

2. Cook the chicken in wok. Once chicken is cooked, remove from wok.

3. Add more oil + cook veggies (1/2 an onion, 2 carrots, 1/2 a bell pepper, 1 jalapeño).

4. Add ~1/4 cup chicken stock. Let it cook down (do this prevent the veggies from sticking to the pan).

5. Add more chicken stock (1 cup).

6. Add spices + *brown sugar* (from thewoksoflife.com).

7. Let the chicken stock cook down a bit, and then add corn starch slurry to thicken sauce. (I added two slurries to get desired thickness; 1 teaspoon corn starch : 2 teaspoons water and another 1:1).

8. Add coconut milk + more spices (if desired).

9. Add the chicken back to the wok and let simmer in sauce for a few minutes.

10. Serve with rice!

Albondigas Soup

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

I really loved this recipe! It reminded me of when my mom would make albondigas soup when I was little. I reccomend making this on a cold/rainy day to get all around cozy vibes. Another good thing about this recipe is that is it pretty inexpensive and it makes A LOT of food (~4.5 servings). I wish the recipe included jalapeños, however, this is very easy to incorperate without directions.

Notes :

1. Substituted the spearmint and parsley in the broth and the oregano in the meatballs for fresh cilantro.

2. Thoroughly chop ~1 cup of cilantro for the meatballs for better incorporation.

3. For the veggies I used carrots, celery, and zucchini.

4. I also substituted the ground beef for ground turkey.

5. Added a few cloves of garlic + some milk to the meatballs.

6. Used equal parts chicken stock to water (1.5 : 1.5 quarts).

7. Added cilantro to the broth ~5 minutes before serving.

8. Added red pepper flakes to the broth.

Lemon Bars

★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

At first I was slightly disappointed with how this came out but I later realized that it was because they had not cooled down all the way when I ate them. These are VERY good and refreshing when they are cold. I reccomdend keeping them in the fridge. Only dust individual pieces you are eating in powdered sugar otherwise it will melt on the the top of all the lemon bars. My main issue with the recipe is that it does not provide a precise measurment for the amount of lemon juice needed. I halved the recipe but still ended up using what I think to be the full amount of lemon juice that the recipe called for (originally very sweet).

Notes :

1. Cook the crust until it is lightly golden (I had to add more time).

2. Add more lemon juice if you want a more tangy lemon bar.

3. If you added more juice, add a couple more spoonsful of flour to it.

4. If using unsalted butter, add a little salt to the crust.

Strawberry Milk

★ ★ ★ ☆ ☆

The recipe in the link provided was a helpful and basic recipe. This allowed me to make alterations to the recipe as I saw fit. I followed the instructions provided in this recipe (with the exception of the ice bath). I just changed the ingredients a little bit. I used half as many strawberries because I did not want that much syrup. Tasted like a fresh flavored milk -- dont expect nesquik! I would like to try this again with malt.

Notes :

1. 1 1/2 cups strawberries; stemmed and chopped

2. 2 tbsp granulated sugar

3. ~1 tsp vanilla extract

4. Pour the syrup in a bowl.

5. Fill a larger bowl with chilled water.

6. Place the syrup filled bowl into the water to cool (ice bath).

7. Blend once cooled.

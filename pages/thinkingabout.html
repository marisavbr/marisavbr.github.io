thinking about : how everyone is a single point in the universe that constantly experiences things and that our interactions with others is just a culmination of the continuous experiences they have had in the context of the continuous experiences that we have had. we experience others as the point while simultaneously experiencing ourselves in the past, present, and future.

Entagled Life : "[...]all life-forms are in fact processes not things. The "you" of five years ago was made from different stuff than the "you" of today. Nature is an event that never stops. As William Bateson, who coined the word genetics, observed, 'We commonly think of animals and plants as matter, but they are really systems through which matter is continually passing.' When we see an organism, from a fungus to a pine tree, we catch a single moment in its continual development."

thinking about : why are humans obsessed with documentation - events, the passing of time, art, etc,.

- it is almost as if we are "archeologists" for our selves. we document what is happening now and this will later be artifacts to ourselves in the future.

- however, "a reliance on artifacts will thus bias an account of ancient hunter-gatherer life"*... or "ancient" events of our own life ⇒ in the future you will not see what you did not choose to document. if you rely too heavily on these artifacts you will have a skewed perception of the past (do not fully rely on this. you cannot document everything nor should you try to).

- think about this when you are documenting or actively not documenting things. ask your self why this might or might not be important for you to have in the future or how might it add to a better self understanding.
